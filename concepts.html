Key Concepts

These terms are important for understanding the relationship between FinTech and cybersecurity.

1. FinTech

FinTech stands for financial technology. It refers to digital tools and systems that improve financial services such as payments, lending, budgeting, investing, and banking.

2. Digital Payments

Digital payments are transactions completed electronically instead of with physical cash. They include online card payments, mobile wallets, and peer-to-peer transfer apps.

3. Encryption

Encryption is the process of converting readable data into coded data so unauthorized users cannot easily access it. It is used to protect payment details and personal financial information.

4. Authentication

Authentication is the process of verifying a user's identity before granting access to an account or system. Examples include passwords, one-time codes, and biometrics.

5. Multi-Factor Authentication

Multi-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring more than one form of verification, such as a password and a phone code.

6. Data Breach

A data breach happens when sensitive information is exposed, stolen, or accessed without permission. In FinTech, this can include customer names, account details, and payment records.

7. Fraud Detection

Fraud detection involves using rules, software, and monitoring tools to identify suspicious activity and stop unauthorized financial transactions.
